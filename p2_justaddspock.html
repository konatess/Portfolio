One of our early in-class assignments was a simple Rock, Paper, Scissors game played against the computer. My group finished with time left over, and just for fun we added Spock and Lizard to our options. Inspired by looking at images for various other game configurations, we searched for a tool to easily create a graphic of our own game, and couldn't find any. I suggested that we build this game generator as an easy way for users to generate games of their own, and then play them with their friends.

I was responsible for a lot of the concept design of the user interface, and for figuring out what math we needed to use to:

  • determine a functional game
  • efficiently test who won in online play
  • dynamically display the elements in a circle

Tassa and I worked together to determine the best structure to store our data so that we could store as little as possible while still having enough to easily recreate the saved game and also use the information for online game play.
